Details about my first semester

My first semester was a whirlwind of new experiences, challenges, and personal growth. Stepping into a new environment filled me with a mix of excitement and nervousness, but it didn’t take long to find my rhythm. From adjusting to a different style of learning to managing my own schedule, every week brought something new to learn—both in and out of the classroom. Academically, I was introduced to subjects that were both intellectually stimulating and, at times, overwhelming. Some courses pushed me out of my comfort zone, but they also taught me discipline, time management, and the importance of asking for help when needed. Beyond the academics, I met people from diverse backgrounds, many of whom I now consider good friends. We shared late-night study sessions, cafeteria meals, and the occasional moment of mutual panic before exams. Looking back, my first semester wasn’t perfect—but it was exactly what I needed to begin this new chapter. It laid the foundation for not only what I want to learn, but who I want to become.


Classes

Communities

One of the most fulfilling parts of my first semester was finding a sense of belonging through the communities I joined. The African community on campus quickly became a space where I felt understood, supported, and empowered. Beyond the cultural events and shared experiences, what stood out most was how willing everyone was to help each other grow—especially when it came to navigating things like academics, networking, and even the job search. Through conversations, referrals, and shared opportunities, I found valuable guidance that helped me take real steps toward my career goals. I also got involved in a few project-based communities, where collaboration and creativity were central. Working with others on real-world challenges helped me apply what I was learning in the classroom and develop practical skills in problem-solving and teamwork. These experiences gave me a glimpse into the professional world and taught me how much can be achieved when people come together with a shared purpose. Together, these communities shaped not just my semester, but my sense of purpose and direction moving forward.
